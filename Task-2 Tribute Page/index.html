Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He was widely regarded as the "Missile Man of India" for his work in ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Early Life and Education

Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering. He graduated from Madras Institute of Technology and joined DRDO and later ISRO.

Scientific Achievements

Dr. Kalam played a major role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 and the development of India's first SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle).

Presidency

He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was known as the "People’s President". He remained committed to education and youth development.

Vision for India

Dr. Kalam envisioned India as a developed country by 2020 and outlined his plan in his book *India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium*.

Legacy

He is remembered for his humility, wisdom, and dedication to public service. His books and speeches continue to inspire millions.
